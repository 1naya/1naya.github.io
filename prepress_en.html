We prepare your files for print!

Your file may not be ready for print. Let us show you the key features of a print-ready file.
 



Correct dimensions:

Digital designers often overlook dimensions, thinking in pixels and providing a 2000px × 2000px JPG file. However, pixels are intended for digital platforms, leaving you uncertain about the appropriate size for printing. Guessing can lead to a final product that doesn't meet your expectations.
Correct file format:

PDF is widely used for various print materials like posters, stickers, magazines, books, and even playing cards. Designers may give you formats like PSD or AI, which require extra preparation before printing and may result in additional charges at the printing shop. At our studio, we offer not only the original file format but also a print-ready PDF. For specific cases like using an EPSON T-shirt printer that accepts only PNG files, we include that too.
Correct bleed and trim marks:

There's a high chance that your designer lacks knowledge of printing and may create files incorrectly. The file should include bleed and trim marks to indicate where the document should be trimmed after printing.
Correct color mode:

For printing, the design file should be in CMYK color mode to ensure accurate color reproduction. RGB color mode is suitable for digital design. This is because monitors and printers perceive colors differently.
Correct resolution:

Sometimes, your design looks great on-screen but appears blurry in actual print. Why does this happen? The file should always have a high resolution, at least 300 pixels per inch, to ensure a sharp and clear printed product.
